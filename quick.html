How Quick Sort Works

Quick Sort is a divide-and-conquer algorithm that works by selecting a 'pivot' element from the array and partitioning the other elements into two sub-arrays according to whether they are less than or greater than the pivot.

The sub-arrays are then recursively sorted. This can be done in-place, requiring small additional amounts of memory to perform the sorting.

Time Complexity: O(n log n) on average, O(n²) in worst case.

Space Complexity: O(log n) due to the recursive call stack.

Color Legend

  • Red - Pivot element
  • Yellow - Elements being compared with pivot
  • Purple - Elements being swapped
