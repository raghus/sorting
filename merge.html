How Merge Sort Works

Merge Sort is a divide-and-conquer algorithm that divides the input array into two halves, recursively sorts them, and then merges the sorted halves.

The key operation is the merging of two sorted subarrays into a single sorted array.

Time Complexity: O(n log n) in all cases (best, average, and worst).

Space Complexity: O(n) as it requires additional space for the temporary arrays during merging.

Color Legend

  • Yellow - Elements being compared
  • Purple - Elements being placed in the merged array
  • Green - Elements in their final sorted position
